# Product Guidelines: Property Tax Grievance Pipeline

## Core Principles
1. **Legal & Professional Tone:** The application must maintain an objective, formal, and trustworthy voice, completely avoiding emotional language. The generated reports are intended for formal review and must reflect that gravity.
2. **Simplicity First with Full Transparency:** Hide complex math until requested to focus the user on the bottom line, but always ensure that all calculations and adjustments are clearly accessible to build trust.
3. **Action-Oriented Guidance:** The application should actively suggest actions (e.g., "Remove this outlier to strengthen your case") rather than passively presenting raw data.

## Design & Formatting
- **Minimalist & Print-Ready:** Focus on clean typography, ample white space, and clear visual hierarchies. The final output must be highly readable and ready to be printed or attached to formal grievance forms (e.g., RP-524).

## Error Handling
- **User-Actionable Errors:** When errors occur (e.g., property not found in Dutchess County API), the system must provide clear, non-technical steps for the user to fix the issue (e.g., 'Check spelling', 'Try using just the street name').
